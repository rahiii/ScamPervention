Learn & Recognize Scams

Overview

Cybercriminals use psychological tricks—urgency, impersonation, and fear—to steal your personal data and money. Stay informed to keep your digital life secure.

Phishing (Email Scams)

Fake emails that mimic trusted companies to steal login info or install malware.

  • Urgency: “Your account is locked – act now!”
  • Impersonation: Logos and sender details that look real.
  • Malicious Attachments: Files that install malware when opened.

Always verify directly with the source before clicking any links.

Smishing (SMS Scams)

Fraudulent text messages designed to lure you into clicking harmful links.

  • Delivery Alerts: “Your package is delayed – reschedule now!”
  • Bank Warnings: “Suspicious login detected – verify immediately.”
  • Prize Notifications: “You’ve won a gift card! Claim it here.”

Be wary of texts from unknown numbers urging instant action.

Vishing (Phone Scams)

Phone calls pretending to be from banks, government, or tech support.

  • Caller ID Spoofing: Calls that appear to be from legitimate sources.
  • Fear Tactics: Claims of compromised accounts or legal trouble.
  • Unusual Payment Methods: Requests for gift cards or crypto.

Never share sensitive details during unsolicited calls. Verify independently.

Social Media Scams

Fake profiles and posts aiming to scam you via giveaways, romance, or investments.

  • Fake Giveaways: Promises of expensive prizes that require personal info.
  • Romance Scams: Long-term deceit to build trust and then ask for money.
  • Investment Scams: Offers of high returns that vanish once you invest.

Double-check profiles and offers using official channels.

Marketplace & Job Scams

Fraudulent listings and job offers on online marketplaces and boards.

  • Fake Listings: Unbelievable deals that never deliver.
  • Job Scams: Offers that require upfront fees for “training.”
  • Overpayment Tricks: Buyers “accidentally” send extra funds.

Always be cautious of deals that seem too good to be true.

How Scammers Operate & Red Flags

Scammers use authority, urgency, and social proof to mislead you. Watch out for:

  • Unexpected requests for sensitive data
  • Messages demanding instant action
  • Links or attachments from unknown sources

When in doubt, verify the request using trusted contact methods.
