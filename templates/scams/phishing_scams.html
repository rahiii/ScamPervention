Phishing & Social Engineering Scams

Scammers don’t always hack your devices — sometimes, they hack your trust. Phishing and social engineering are manipulative tactics that cybercriminals use to trick people into giving away sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or bank details.

What is Phishing?

Phishing is when a scammer pretends to be a trustworthy source — like a bank, school, or even a friend — to trick you into clicking a link or sharing personal info.

  • Emails: “Your account has been suspended, click here to reactivate.”
  • Text messages: “You’ve won a prize!”
  • Fake websites: mimic real ones but are designed to steal your data

What is Social Engineering?

Social engineering is psychological manipulation to trick people into giving up confidential info. Think of it as human hacking.

  • Fake customer support calls
  • Online friendships that lead to asking for money or secrets
  • Urgent requests that pressure you into quick action

How to Stay Safe

  1. Think Before You Click: Suspicious links? Don’t touch.
  2. Verify Sources: Contact companies directly, not through the message.
  3. Watch for Errors: Spelling mistakes and weird URLs are red flags.
  4. Use MFA: Multi-Factor Authentication protects even if your password leaks.
  5. Never Share Sensitive Info: Legit organizations won’t ask for passwords over email or text.

Always double-check messages with the source — never trust unknown links.
