  • Official resources from agencies for cybersecurity awareness. (For example, the FTC’s Consumer Advice on scams, the FBI’s Common Scams page, and CISA’s Security Tips.)

Educational Resources from Cybersecurity Organizations

  • Free learning materials from non-profits and industry groups. (See the National Cybersecurity Alliance’s Stay Safe Online or SANS Institute’s OUCH! newsletters.)

Report a Scam

  • Report to Email Providers – Learn how to flag phishing in Gmail or Outlook/Hotmail.
  • Report Scams to Authorities – File a report with the FTC’s fraud reporting site, Action Fraud in the UK, or the Canadian Anti-Fraud Centre.
  • Crowdsourced Scam Databases – Submit phishing examples to public databases like PhishTank.
